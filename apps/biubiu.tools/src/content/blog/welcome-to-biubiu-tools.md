This is the first post on the BiuBiu blog, so it's worth saying plainly what the
project is and why it exists.

**BiuBiu Tools** is a collection of small, focused utilities for doing on-chain
work without the usual friction. Check what can still move your tokens. Scan
balances across thousands of addresses. Send to a long list of recipients in one
signature. Browse hundreds of networks and add one to your wallet in a click.
Each tool does one thing, runs in your browser, and tries to make a tedious or
risky task feel calm and obvious.

## The ideas behind it

A few principles shaped almost every decision:

- **Self-custodial.** You hold your keys. We can't move, freeze, or recover your
  funds — and we designed it so we never have to.
- **Privacy-first.** The sensitive parts of your work happen on your device.
  Many tools need no account at all.
- **No blind signing.** If a tool submits a transaction, it shows you what you're
  approving in plain language first.
- **Free and open.** The whole thing is open source, so you don't have to take
  our word for any of the above.

## What we'll write about here

Roughly three kinds of posts:

- **Release notes** — what shipped and what changed. Short and honest.
- **Guides** — how to get the most out of a specific tool, with the gotchas we
  wish someone had told us.
- **Build notes** — the engineering decisions and trade-offs behind the toolkit.
